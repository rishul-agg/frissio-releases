Questions

How is this different from other expense trackers?
Most expense trackers ask you to upload your statements, or hand over your bank credentials. Frissio does neither. Your data is parsed on your Mac, and the app has no way to send it anywhere.
Can I use this to get ready for ITR filing?
Yes. Frissio totals your income, investments, and expenses for the financial year, and lets you tag the spending that tends to matter at filing time, rent, insurance, home loan, donations, then exports a clean summary for your CA. It is not a filing tool and gives no tax advice. It sums what you confirm, and never calculates your tax or tells you what you can claim.
What does Frissio figure out on its own?
Whether a transaction is income, an investment, or an expense. The category it belongs to (Food, Transport, Housing, and the rest). And the merchant behind a noisy bank description.
How do I trust that nothing leaves my Mac?
The simplest way: open Activity Monitor while using Frissio. Sent and received bytes stay at zero. For the technical version: the signed app does not declare network access in its entitlements, so macOS itself blocks any outgoing connection at the OS level. It is not a promise. It is enforced by Apple.
Which banks are supported?
HDFC and ICICI bank statements and credit cards are recognized natively, along with Axis and American Express cards. Other banks work via CSV import, which most net banking portals export directly. If your bank is not listed yet, the CSV path still gets you to the same dashboard.
Does it cost anything?
Free during early access. Pricing comes later. Anyone who downloads now stays free even after pricing launches.
Will there be a Windows or iOS version?
Mac only for now. The privacy guarantees lean on macOS sandboxing, so other platforms would need to be designed differently. Not on the near-term roadmap.
What do I need to run it?
A Mac running macOS 12 (Monterey) or later. Works on both Apple Silicon and Intel.
